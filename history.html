HISTORY OF EUROVISION

From the first contest to modern spectacular finals — key winners, milestones, and unforgettable moments from Eurovision history.

🎤 The Beginning:
1956

  • The first Eurovision Song Contest was held in Lugano, Switzerland.
  • Only seven countries participated in the debut edition.
  • Switzerland won with Lys Assia performing “Refrain”.
  • The contest became a symbol of post-war European unity.
1956

🏆 Iconic Winners:
Golden Moments

  • ABBA’s “Waterloo” made Sweden a global pop powerhouse in 1974.
  • Johnny Logan became one of the contest’s most famous champions.
  • Alexander Rybak set a record with “Fairytale” in 2009.
  • Loreen’s “Euphoria” became one of Eurovision’s defining songs.
Legends

🌍 Eurovision Grows:
Expansion Era

  • More countries joined as the contest expanded across Europe.
  • Broadcast technology brought bigger stages and bigger audiences.
  • Voting systems evolved from juries to televoting and hybrid formats.
  • The contest became one of the world’s largest live music events.
Growth

✨ Modern Era:
Spectacle & Sound

  • Eurovision became a platform for bold staging and genre diversity.
  • Entries began combining performance art, pop culture, and politics.
  • Memorable hosts, interval acts, and production design raised the bar.
  • Each year adds new viral moments and historic performances.
Today
